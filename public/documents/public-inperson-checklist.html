Public School In-Person Book Fair Checklist

Your step-by-step guide to a successful in-person book fair

STEP 1 You Did It!

  • Choose Ignatius Book Fairs and set your fair dates You've already accomplished this step! Check out our Resources throughout this guide.

STEP 2 Design Your Book Fair Experience

  • Give your school community advance notice of the book fair schedule so they can plan their visit
  • Share the Backpack Flyer the week before your fair opens
  • Keep your fair open one or more evenings and/or over the weekend Consider hosting an evening event with snacks, beverages, and socializing
  • Consider being open all weekend and inviting people outside your school — be creative with your events!
  • Extend advertising to homeschoolers, co-ops, and families at neighboring schools

STEP 3 Recruit Volunteers

  • Create an online sign-up using a free service like SignUpGenius
  • Recruit volunteers to help with: promoting the fair within the school, unboxing and setup, scanning in boxes, decorating the space, working the floor suggesting books, running the check-out station, organizing refreshments for evening/weekend events, running a craft table for kids, promoting on social media, and packing up

STEP 4 Set Up Your Space

  • Arrange tables away from walls in a space of at least 800 sq ft
  • Plan for 12–16 six-foot tables (or equivalent):
    Early Readers1 table
    Picture Books2–3 tables
    Elementary Books3 tables
    Middle School Books1–3 tables
    Seasonal Books1 table
    Crafts & Activities1 table
    Comic Books1 table
    Older Readers & Adults1 table
    Toys & Trinkets1 table
    Check-out Station1–2 tables
  • Plan additional tables for snacks/beverages or a coloring page station for younger kids
  • Have on hand: bags for purchases, a cashbox for cash sales, a Sharpie, packing tape

STEP 5 Calendars & Scheduling

  • Add fair details (times, dates, location) to all necessary school calendars
  • Use our Advertising Resources for help with graphics

STEP 6 Advertise Early & Often

  • Post a Save-the-Date announcement 4–6 weeks before the fair
  • Advertise weekly leading up to opening day using our Advertising Resources
  • During the fair, post photos of what's available
  • Before the last day, remind your community of their last chance to shop
  • Places to advertise: school bulletin, calendar, and email list; announcement at school and public events; school newsletter from the principal or coordinator; school website, Facebook, Instagram, X; library flyers; announcements to homeschoolers, co-ops, and area schools; neighborhood paper

STEP 7 Watch Our Training Workshop

  • Watch our workshop video series featuring task-specific topics to keep you on pace: Part 1 • Part 2
  • Invite your volunteer team to watch along

STEP 8 Receive Your Book Fair Supplies

  • One week before your fair, we deliver: all books and items for sale, 2 payment devices, table category signs in acrylic frames, 50 book stands, colorful tablecloths (mint, yellow, orange), and 5 decorative posters. Add your own flair with seasonal themes, student art, balloons, or twinkle lights!

STEP 9 Delivery & Set-Up

  • Confirm receipt of 20–38 boxes delivered one week before your fair
  • Find the box marked Open First — plug in payment devices to charge
  • Find the marketing box and set up tablecloths, category signs, and book stands
  • Log in to the payment device with your unique code and scan in the boxes For POS device instructions, click here
  • Sort boxes by category, place in front of corresponding tables, and unbox
  • Arrange books for maximum viewing — bring tables away from walls

Advertising Schedule

  • 6 weeks before: Post Save the Date on all social media, school bulletin, and newsletter
  • 5 weeks before: Share this video on social media; include sign-up link for parents to create accounts
  • 4 weeks before: Post another Save the Date reminder with this graphic
  • 3 weeks before: Highlight select titles your staff or teachers enjoy; explain how sales benefit the school
  • 2 weeks before: Have principal send an invitation to the community with fair schedule
  • 1 week before: Set out Sneak Peek flyers in common areas and school newsletter
  • Evening before: Post kickoff graphic on social media Videos: How to Create a Parent Account • How Orders Benefit Your School

During the Fair (Daily Posts)

  • Day 1: Kick off with a reading challenge: commit to setting aside dedicated family reading time
  • Day 2: Post this graphic; remind community about in-person times/location + online ordering
  • Day 3: Share reading recommendations by age and grade level
  • Day 4: Share the top 5 most popular titles so far
  • Day 5: Encourage "Stop, Drop and Read" — 20 min daily family reading habit
  • Day 6: Thank everyone; ask parents to share their favorite picks
  • Day 7: Last day — final reminder that purchases earn rewards back for the school

FINAL Have a Wonderful Book Fair!

Reach out to your Book Fair Manager if any issues arise — we're here to cheer you on and give you advice and support along the way!
